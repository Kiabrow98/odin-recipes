Brisket Recipe

Description:

If you want melt in your mouth, smoky perfection, this Texas-style smoked brisket recipe is a fan favorite with neartly perfect ratings. It's simple, bold, and lets the beef shine through.

Ingredients:

  • Whole packer brisket (10-14 lbs)
  • 2 tbsp coarse kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp coarse black pepper
  • 2 tbsp garlic powder

Equipment:

  • Smoker
  • Butcher paper or foil
  • Meat thermometer
  • Sharp slicing knife

Instructions:

  1. Trim the brisket
    Keep about ¼” fat cap.
    Remove silver skin and excess fat for even cooking.
  2. Season generously
    Mix salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
    Coat all sides of the brisket.
  3. Smoke low and slow
    Set smoker to 225°F.
    Place brisket fat-side down, point end toward heat source.
    Smoke until internal temp hits 165°F (about 8 hours).
  4. Wrap it up
    Use butcher paper or foil to wrap tightly.
    Return to smoker, seam-side down.
  5. Finish and cook
    Continue smoking until internal temp reaches 202°F (5-8 more hours).
  6. Rest and slice
    Let brisket rest for 1 hour.
    Slice against the grain and serve.

Serving Tips:

Pair it with classic sides like potato salad, coleslaw, or jalapeño cornbread. And if you want burnt ends from the point cut all you have to do is cube, re-season, and smoke a bit longer.
